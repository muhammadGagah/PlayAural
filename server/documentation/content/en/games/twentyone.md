\*\*Twenty One\*\*



Twenty One (Survival Rules) is a two-player card game where you try to get closer to a target number — usually 21 — than your opponent without going over. Unlike standard blackjack, this is a survival game: each player has hit points, and you play round after round until someone runs out.



\*\*Gameplay\*\*



The game is played in rounds. Each round, both players are dealt two cards from a deck numbered 1 through 11. Your first card is your hidden hole card, visible only to you. Your second card is face-up for both players to see.



On your turn you may hit (draw another face-up card), stand (lock in your total), or play a change card from your hand. Hitting has a chance to award you an additional random change card.



A round ends when both players stand. Totals are then compared against the current target. The player closer to the target without going over wins. If one player busts (exceeds the target) and the other does not, the non-busted player wins. If both bust, whoever is closer to the target wins. If totals are exactly tied, both players take damage.



\*\*Important:\*\* if either player hits or plays a change card after one player has already stood, that standing player's stand is cancelled and they must act again. You cannot safely lock in and ignore what your opponent does.



The loser of a round takes damage equal to the current bet value. The bet starts at the base bet (default 1) and can be raised or reduced by change-card effects. When a player's HP reaches zero, they are eliminated and the other player wins the game.



At the start of each round, both players receive a number of random change cards (default 1 per round). Each time you hit and draw a card there is also a chance (default 35 percent) of receiving an additional random change card.



\*\*Card Types\*\*



The deck contains cards ranked 1 through 11, with one copy of each rank per deck. There are no suits. A fresh deck is shuffled at the start of every round. The host can configure multiple deck copies to be shuffled together, which means higher cards may appear more than once and the deck becomes harder to read.



\*\*Change Cards\*\*



Change cards are the tactical heart of the game. On your turn, instead of hitting or standing, you may play one change card from your hand. Some are instant effects that resolve immediately and are discarded. Others are table effects that persist for the rest of the round. Each player can have up to 5 table effects active at once; if you add a sixth, your oldest effect is removed.



Press \*\*C\*\* or select \*\*Change Card Guide\*\* from the actions menu to open a status box listing every change card and its description. The description is also read aloud whenever a change card is selected from the play menu.



\*\*Stake Modifiers (table effects):\*\*

\* \*\*Raise one:\*\* Increase opponent damage by 1 for the round. Gain 1 change card.

\* \*\*Raise two:\*\* Increase opponent damage by 2 for the round. Gain 1 change card.

\* \*\*Withdraw and raise two:\*\* Increase opponent damage by 2, return your opponent's last drawn face-up card to the top of the deck, and gain 1 change card.



\*\*Exact Draw (instant):\*\*

\* \*\*Draw 2 through Draw 7:\*\* Search the deck for a card of that exact rank and add it to your hand. If no card of that rank remains, the change card is wasted.



\*\*Card Manipulation (instant):\*\*

\* \*\*Withdraw:\*\* Remove your opponent's last drawn face-up card and place it on top of the deck.

\* \*\*Undraw:\*\* Return your own last drawn face-up card to the top of the deck.

\* \*\*Swap top cards:\*\* Exchange your last drawn face-up card with your opponent's last drawn face-up card.



\*\*Change Card Management (instant):\*\*

\* \*\*Change-up:\*\* Discard 2 of your change cards, then receive 3 new random change cards.

\* \*\*Change-up enhanced:\*\* Discard 1 of your change cards, then receive 4 new random change cards.

\* \*\*Change is good:\*\* Both players gain 1 change card.

\* \*\*Glitched draw:\*\* Discard 1 random change card; force your opponent to draw the highest available number card. Requires at least 1 other change card to pay the cost.



\*\*Defense (table effects):\*\*

\* \*\*Defend:\*\* Reduce incoming round damage by 1.

\* \*\*Defend enhanced:\*\* Reduce incoming round damage by 2.

\* \*\*Risky change:\*\* Gain 3 change cards immediately; your own incoming damage increases by 1 while active.

\* \*\*Run away!:\*\* If you would lose a round, this effect is consumed and you take no damage instead.



\*\*Disruption (instant and table effects):\*\*

\* \*\*Delete:\*\* Destroy your opponent's newest table effect (instant).

\* \*\*Delete enhanced:\*\* Destroy all of your opponent's table effects (instant).

\* \*\*Delete double enhanced:\*\* Clear all opponent table effects and prevent your opponent from playing any change cards for the rest of the round (table effect).

\* \*\*Defensive offense:\*\* Requires at least 3 of your own defend effects active. Removes up to 3 defend effects from your table; opponent damage +3 while active.

\* \*\*Defensive offense enhanced:\*\* Requires at least 2 of your own defend effects active. Removes up to 2 defend effects; opponent damage +5 while active.

\* \*\*Cost of change:\*\* While active, opponent damage increases by half the number of change cards in their hand (table effect).

\* \*\*Cost of change enhanced:\*\* While active, opponent damage increases by their full change-card count (table effect).

\* \*\*Change for change's sake:\*\* At round end, opponent discards half their change cards. Expires early if the opponent plays 2 change cards in one turn (table effect).

\* \*\*Change for change's sake enhanced:\*\* At round end, opponent discards all their change cards. Expires early if the opponent plays 3 change cards in one turn (table effect).



\*\*Precision Drawing (instant and table effects):\*\*

\* \*\*Best draw:\*\* Search the deck and draw the best available card for reaching the current target without going over. If already at or above the target, draws the card that gets you closest.

\* \*\*Best draw and raise five:\*\* Best draw, and also places a table effect that increases opponent damage by 5 for the round. Very powerful.

\* \*\*Best draw with change:\*\* Best draw, and gain 2 change cards.

\* \*\*Dark bargain:\*\* Discard half your change cards, then draw your best card; increases opponent damage by 10 while active (table effect). Requires at least 2 other change cards.



\*\*Target Modifiers (table effects):\*\*

\* \*\*Target 17:\*\* Set the round target to 17 for both players.

\* \*\*Target 24:\*\* Set the round target to 24 for both players.

\* \*\*Target 27:\*\* Set the round target to 27 for both players.

\* Only one target modifier can be in effect at once. Playing a new target modifier removes the existing one.



\*\*Other:\*\*

\* \*\*Embrace change:\*\* Whenever any change card is played, you gain 1 change card. This triggers for every change card played while active (table effect).

\* \*\*Trojan horse:\*\* Your opponent draws their best available card for the current target. This helps your opponent, not you. You may receive it randomly — use change-up to cycle it out.

\* \*\*21 at 21:\*\* While your hand total is exactly 21, opponent damage increases by 21 (table effect).

\* \*\*Nope:\*\* Cancel the current round immediately and start a new one (instant).

\* \*\*No draw for you!:\*\* While active, your opponent cannot draw number cards from hits or change-card effects (table effect).

\* \*\*Game over:\*\* While active, both players' damage is increased by 100 and your opponent cannot draw number cards (table effect).



\*\*Game Options\*\*



The host can configure these settings at the table before starting:

\* \*\*Starting Health:\*\* How much HP each player begins with. Default 10, range 1 to 100.

\* \*\*Base Bet:\*\* The base damage dealt to the round loser before modifier adjustments. Default 1, range 0 to 50.

\* \*\*Starting Change Cards Per Round:\*\* How many random change cards each player receives at the start of each round. Default 1, range 0 to 10.

\* \*\*Draw Change Card Chance:\*\* The percentage chance of gaining a random change card each time you hit. Default 35 percent, range 0 to 100 percent.

\* \*\*Deck Count:\*\* How many copies of the 1-through-11 deck to shuffle together each round. Default 1, range 1 to 10; one deck means 11 cards total. More decks mean more cards and less predictability.



\*\*Example Round\*\*



Both players start with 10 HP. The base bet is 1 and the target is 21.

You are dealt a hidden 7 and a face-up 9. Your total is 16. Your opponent shows a face-up 5; their hole card is unknown.

You also receive a random change card: Defend.

It is your turn. You decide to hit and draw a 3, bringing your total to 19. You also receive a random change card from the draw: Raise one.

You play Raise one as a table effect. Your opponent will now take 2 damage instead of 1 if they lose this round. Playing the change card also awards you another random change card.

Your opponent hits and draws a 6. Their face-up cards now show a total of 11. They stand.

You are happy with 19, so you stand as well. Both players have stood; the round resolves.

Your opponent reveals their hole card: a 4. Their total is 15. You are closer to 21 without busting, so you win the round.

Your opponent takes 2 damage (base bet 1, plus 1 from your Raise one). They drop to 8 HP. A new round begins.



\*\*Keyboard Shortcuts\*\*



\* \*\*1:\*\* Hit (draw a card).

\* \*\*2:\*\* Stand (lock in your total).

\* \*\*3:\*\* Play Change Card (opens a menu to choose which change card to play).

\* \*\*4:\*\* Check 21 status (announces target, HP, current bet, your hand, change cards in hand, and active effects; also shows your opponent's visible information).

\* \*\*C:\*\* Change Card Guide (opens a status box listing every change card and its description).

\* \*\*O:\*\* Read opponent face-up cards (announces what your opponent is showing and their visible total).

\* \*\*R:\*\* Read current hand (announces your cards and total).

\* \*\*W:\*\* Read current bets (announces the effective bet for each player after modifier adjustments).

\* \*\*E:\*\* Read active effects (announces the table effects active for both players).

\* \*\*S:\*\* Check scores (shows current HP for both players).

\* \*\*Shift+S:\*\* Detailed scores.
