\*\*Nine\*\*



Nine is a Russian card game in which players race to empty their hand by building four suit sequences on the table, one for each suit.



\*\*Gameplay\*\*



Nine uses a 36-card deck containing ranks Six through Ace in each of the four suits. Five players is not supported; the game seats 2, 3, 4, or 6 players.



Cards are dealt evenly at the start: 18 cards each for 2 players, 12 each for 3, 9 each for 4, and 6 each for 6.



The player holding the nine of clubs always goes first and must open the game by playing it. After that, play proceeds in turn order.



On your turn you may do one of the following:

\* \*\*Play a nine:\*\* If you hold a nine of any suit whose sequence has not yet been started, you may play it to begin that suit's sequence on the table.

\* \*\*Extend a sequence:\*\* Play any card that sits exactly one rank higher or one rank lower than the current ends of an existing sequence in that suit. Sequences grow outward in both directions from the nine toward Six at the low end and Ace at the high end.

\* \*\*Skip:\*\* If neither option is available to you, your turn is skipped automatically. You cannot skip while any valid play exists.



The first player to play all of their cards wins. At the end of the game, remaining players are ranked by how many cards they still hold.



\*\*Example Turn\*\*



It is Alice's turn. The clubs sequence currently runs Eight through Ten, and no hearts sequence has been started yet. Alice holds the Seven of Clubs, the Nine of Hearts, and the Jack of Diamonds.



Alice cannot play the Jack of Diamonds because there is no diamonds sequence yet and it is not a nine. She can play the Seven of Clubs to extend the clubs sequence downward, or play the Nine of Hearts to start the hearts sequence.



She plays the Nine of Hearts, opening the hearts sequence on the table. Her turn ends and play passes to the next player.



\*\*Game Options\*\*



Nine has no game-specific options.



\*\*Keyboard Shortcuts\*\*



\* \*\*C:\*\* Check the current state of all four suit sequences on the table.

\* \*\*E:\*\* Check how many cards each player is holding.

\* \*\*T:\*\* Check whose turn it is.

\* \*\*Ctrl+U:\*\* Check who is at the table.
