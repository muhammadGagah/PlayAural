\*\*Dead Man's Poker\*\*

Dead Man's Poker is a survival poker game. It uses normal poker hand rankings, but it does not use chips, money, or points. Every bet is a bullet. If you fold or lose a showdown, the bullets you committed are loaded into the revolver.

If you have never played poker before, start with this idea: you are trying to make the strongest 5-card hand from your own 2 private cards and the shared cards on the table. The game reads the important information aloud, so you can learn the hand types while playing.

\*\*Goal\*\*

Be the last surviving player at the table.

You do not win by collecting chips. You win by making good decisions, surviving the revolver, and outlasting everyone else.

\*\*Players\*\*

Dead Man's Poker supports 2 to 4 players.

Players who are eliminated no longer receive cards or take turns. The match continues until only one player is still alive.

\*\*Cards\*\*

The game uses one standard 52-card deck.

Each hand uses:

\* \*\*Private cards:\*\* each living player receives 2 private cards. Only you hear your own private cards until showdown.
\* \*\*Community cards:\*\* 5 shared cards are placed on the table. Everyone can use these cards.

Your final poker hand is the best possible 5-card hand made from any combination of your 2 private cards and the 5 community cards. You do not have to use both private cards. Sometimes your best hand uses one private card. Sometimes it uses only the community cards.

\*\*Card Order\*\*

For poker hand strength, the ranks go from low to high like this:

\* 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, jack, queen, king, ace.

An ace is usually the highest card. In a straight only, an ace can also be low in A-2-3-4-5.

Suits do not have strength by themselves. Hearts are not stronger than clubs, clubs are not stronger than spades, and so on. Suits matter only for flushes and straight flushes.

\*\*Poker Hand Rankings\*\*

From strongest to weakest, poker hands are:

\* \*\*Royal flush:\*\* 10, jack, queen, king, and ace, all the same suit. Example: 10 hearts, jack hearts, queen hearts, king hearts, ace hearts. This is the strongest possible straight flush.
\* \*\*Straight flush:\*\* 5 cards in a row, all the same suit. Example: 6 spades, 7 spades, 8 spades, 9 spades, 10 spades.
\* \*\*Four of a kind:\*\* 4 cards of the same rank, plus one other card. Example: four queens and a 7.
\* \*\*Full house:\*\* 3 cards of one rank plus 2 cards of another rank. Example: three kings and two 4s.
\* \*\*Flush:\*\* 5 cards of the same suit, not in order. Example: 2 diamonds, 6 diamonds, 9 diamonds, jack diamonds, ace diamonds.
\* \*\*Straight:\*\* 5 cards in a row, not all the same suit. Example: 8, 9, 10, jack, queen. A-2-3-4-5 is the lowest straight.
\* \*\*Three of a kind:\*\* 3 cards of the same rank, plus two other cards. Example: three 9s.
\* \*\*Two pair:\*\* 2 cards of one rank and 2 cards of another rank, plus one other card. Example: two aces and two 6s.
\* \*\*One pair:\*\* 2 cards of the same rank, plus three other cards. Example: two kings.
\* \*\*High card:\*\* no pair, no straight, no flush, and no stronger hand. The highest card decides the hand.

If two players have the same type of hand, the higher ranks decide the winner. For example, a pair of kings beats a pair of queens. If the main ranks are equal, the next highest extra cards decide. If the best hands are exactly tied, those players tie for the best hand.

The game announces this strongest hand as Royal Flush.

\*\*Hand Setup\*\*

At the start of each hand:

\* Every surviving player automatically commits 1 bullet.
\* The deck is shuffled.
\* Each surviving player receives 2 private cards.
\* Five community cards arrive face down.
\* Betting round 1 begins with no community cards revealed.

The first player changes from hand to hand. The first hand starts from a random living player, then the first actor rotates so the same player does not always move first.

\*\*The Four Betting Rounds\*\*

Each hand has 4 betting rounds:

\* \*\*Round 1:\*\* no community cards are revealed.
\* \*\*Round 2:\*\* the first 3 community cards are revealed.
\* \*\*Round 3:\*\* 1 more community card is revealed.
\* \*\*Round 4:\*\* the final community card is revealed.

During a normal betting round, each active player gets one decision. When everyone still active has acted, the game moves to the next reveal or to showdown.

\*\*Call\*\*

Call means you stay in the hand and place 1 more bullet on the table.

The more bullets you commit, the more dangerous the revolver becomes if you later fold or lose the showdown.

\*\*Fold\*\*

Fold means you leave the current hand.

Folding does not protect you for free. You must face the revolver using the number of bullets you already committed in that hand. If you survive, you stay alive for the next hand.

On your first decision of a hand, the Fold button changes into Coward's Fold while you still have only the opening bullet committed. If you have already used Coward's Fold earlier in the match, the same button stays in that context and tells you why you cannot use it again.

\*\*Coward's Fold\*\*

Coward's Fold is a special safety action.

You may use it only once per match, and only on your first decision in a hand. It lets you leave immediately while risking exactly 1 bullet.

Use it when your opening cards look terrible and you do not want the danger to grow. After you have used Coward's Fold once, you cannot use it again for the rest of the match.

\*\*Switch Card\*\*

Switch Card can be used once per hand.

You choose one of your two private cards. The game then offers three replacement cards. Choose one replacement, and that card becomes part of your private hand.

Switching does not spend your betting decision. After the switch finishes, it is still your turn and you must choose Call, Fold, or All-in.

Other players are told exactly which card you discarded. They are not told which replacement card you chose.

You cannot switch after the final community card is revealed, and you cannot switch while responding to an all-in.

\*\*All-In\*\*

All-in means you push your committed bullets up to 8.

All-in is available only from betting round 2 onward, after the first three community cards have been revealed. The opening round is for the first commitment decisions, so players cannot immediately force the table to 8 bullets before anyone sees the flop.

When someone goes all-in, normal betting stops. Every other active player must choose:

\* \*\*Match all-in:\*\* raise their own committed bullets to 8 and continue to showdown.
\* \*\*Fold:\*\* leave the hand and face the revolver with their current bullets.

You can match an all-in with either Call or All-in. Both buttons perform the same match-all-in response during this moment.

If every opponent folds, the all-in player wins the hand without showdown.

\*\*Showdown\*\*

If more than one player remains after the fourth betting round, the game goes to showdown.

The game reveals any remaining community cards first. Then it reveals each active player's private cards one player at a time. The strongest poker hand wins the showdown.

All players below the best hand lose the showdown and must face the revolver with their committed bullets. If all remaining active players are tied for the best hand, nobody faces the revolver and a new hand begins.

\*\*The Revolver\*\*

The revolver risk is based on the number of bullets you committed in the current hand.

If you face the revolver:

\* 1 to 7 bullets means the death chance is that many bullets out of 8.
\* 8 bullets means a 95 percent death chance.
\* At 8 bullets, there is still a 5 percent survival chance.

If the chamber is empty, you survive and your committed bullets are cleared. If the gun fires, you are eliminated from the match.

\*\*Information Actions\*\*

Use the information actions whenever you need orientation:

\* \*\*Read hand:\*\* hear your private cards.
\* \*\*Read hand strength:\*\* hear your current best hand estimate.
\* \*\*Read community cards:\*\* hear the revealed table cards and how many community cards are still face down.
\* \*\*Read table:\*\* hear the hand number, betting round, community cards, current turn, and player states.
\* \*\*Read revolvers:\*\* hear each player's committed bullets and current death risk.
\* \*\*Whose turn:\*\* hear the current turn.
\* \*\*Who's at table:\*\* hear the table roster.

\*\*Winning\*\*

There are no scores and no score target.

The match ends when only one player is still alive. That player wins Dead Man's Poker.

\*\*Customizable Options\*\*

Dead Man's Poker currently uses fixed rules. The host has no game-specific options.

\*\*Keyboard Shortcuts\*\*

\* \*\*C:\*\* Call, or match all-in when responding to an all-in.
\* \*\*F:\*\* Fold, or Coward's Fold when that is the current fold context.
\* \*\*D:\*\* Switch card.
\* \*\*Shift+A:\*\* All-in, or match all-in when responding to an all-in.
\* \*\*W:\*\* Read your hand.
\* \*\*G:\*\* Read your current hand strength.
\* \*\*E:\*\* Read community cards.
\* \*\*V:\*\* Read the full table status.
\* \*\*P:\*\* Read revolver risk.
\* \*\*T:\*\* Check whose turn it is.
\* \*\*Ctrl+U:\*\* Hear who is at the table.
