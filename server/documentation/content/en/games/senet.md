\*\*Senet\*\*

Senet is an ancient Egyptian board game, quite possibly the oldest board game still played today, with origins reaching back more than five thousand years. Boards have been found in tombs across Egypt, and the pharaoh Tutankhamun was buried with several of his own. Two players race five pieces each along a thirty-square S-shaped track, throwing sticks instead of dice and trying to bear all of their pieces off the board before the opponent can.

Senet is played by \*\*exactly two players\*\*.

\*\*Starting Position\*\*

Each player begins with five pieces, placed alternately along the first ten squares:

\* Player 1: squares 1, 3, 5, 7, and 9.
\* Player 2: squares 2, 4, 6, 8, and 10.

The two roles are assigned randomly at the start of the game, but Player 1 always throws first.

\*\*Board Layout\*\*

The board is a grid of three rows by ten columns. Pieces travel along an S-shaped path that runs the length of each row in turn:

\* Row 1 (top): squares 1 to 10, left to right.
\* Row 2 (middle): squares 11 to 20, right to left.
\* Row 3 (bottom): squares 21 to 30, left to right.

Both players travel the same direction, from square 1 toward square 30. Because the board is laid out as a physical grid, the middle row reads in reverse: square 20 sits at the left edge and square 11 at the right, reflecting the back-and-forth of the path.

\*\*Throwing the Sticks\*\*

Senet uses four throwing sticks rather than dice. Each stick has a marked side and a blank side, and your move is decided by how many land marked-side up. On your turn, press Enter on any square to throw:

\* \*\*1 marked:\*\* move 1 square, and throw again (bonus throw).
\* \*\*2 marked:\*\* move 2 squares.
\* \*\*3 marked:\*\* move 3 squares.
\* \*\*4 marked:\*\* move 4 squares, and throw again (bonus throw).
\* \*\*0 marked (all blank):\*\* move 5 squares, and throw again (bonus throw).

A roll of 1, 4, or 5 earns a bonus throw: you make your move for the current roll, then immediately throw again. Bonus throws chain, so a run of bonus-granting rolls can carry a piece a long way in a single turn.

\*\*Moving Pieces\*\*

After throwing, select one of your own pieces to move it. Each piece has at most one legal destination for a given roll, so selecting a piece moves it straight there. The rules of movement are:

\* Pieces only move forward, toward square 30.
\* You may pass over pieces that lie in your path.
\* You cannot land on a square already holding one of your own pieces.
\* If you land on a square holding a single opponent piece, the two pieces \*\*swap\*\*: yours takes the destination, and the opponent's piece falls back to the square you left.
\* If you have no legal move for a roll, you forfeit the move, but you still take any bonus throw the roll earned.

\*\*Protection and Blocking\*\*

Two or more of your pieces on adjacent squares form a protected group. Protected pieces \*\*cannot be captured\*\* — an opponent may not land on, and so cannot swap with, a piece that has a neighbour of its own colour.

A run of three or more consecutive opponent pieces forms an impassable wall: you cannot pass over such a formation, and any move that would carry a piece across it is illegal.

\*\*Special Squares\*\*

Five squares carry special meaning:

\* \*\*Square 15 — House of Rebirth:\*\* the square to which pieces sent back from the House of Water return. It has no other effect.
\* \*\*Square 26 — House of Happiness:\*\* every piece \*\*must\*\* land here on its way home. You cannot pass over square 26, so a roll that would carry a piece beyond it without stopping is not a legal move. Once a piece has rested on square 26, it moves on normally on a later turn.
\* \*\*Square 27 — House of Water:\*\* landing here is misfortune. The piece is sent straight back to the House of Rebirth (square 15). If square 15 is occupied, the piece goes to the nearest empty square before it. Even a piece that arrives by swapping onto the water is sent back this way.
\* \*\*Square 28 — House of Three Truths:\*\* a piece that reaches here is locked safely in place and can leave only by bearing off with an exact roll of \*\*3\*\*.
\* \*\*Square 29 — House of Re-Atum:\*\* as square 28, but the piece bears off only on an exact roll of \*\*2\*\*.

Square 30 behaves the same way: a piece there is safe and bears off only on an exact roll of \*\*1\*\*. Pieces resting on squares 28, 29, and 30 are all immune from capture.

\*\*Bearing Off\*\*

Pieces leave the board only from the three final squares, and each demands its exact roll:

\* From square 28: exactly 3.
\* From square 29: exactly 2.
\* From square 30: exactly 1.

No piece may bear off from any other square. Pieces must reach these final squares and wait there for the right throw.

\*\*Winning\*\*

The first player to bear off all five of their pieces wins the game.

\*\*Game Options\*\*

\* \*\*Bot Difficulty:\*\* Default Simple. Choices: Simple (the bot weighs its moves with a heuristic) or Random (the bot plays a random legal move).

\*\*Keyboard Shortcuts\*\*

\* \*\*Enter (on any grid square):\*\* Throw the sticks before throwing, or move the selected piece during the movement phase.
\* \*\*Ctrl+Down or Ctrl+Right:\*\* Cycle forward through your pieces that have a legal move.
\* \*\*Ctrl+Up or Ctrl+Left:\*\* Cycle backward through your pieces that have a legal move.
\* \*\*E:\*\* Read game status — pieces borne off, the current phase, and the current roll.
\* \*\*C:\*\* Read the result of the current stick throw.
\* \*\*S:\*\* Read the score, the count of pieces each player has borne off.
\* \*\*T:\*\* Check whose turn it is.

\*\*Strategy Tips\*\*

\* \*\*Clear the House of Happiness early.\*\* Every piece must stop on square 26, so it is a bottleneck. Getting pieces through it sooner leaves you more freedom later.
\* \*\*Steer clear of the water.\*\* Square 27 hurls a piece all the way back to square 15. Plan around it, especially for pieces that have already travelled far.
\* \*\*Advance in pairs.\*\* Two adjacent pieces shield each other from capture. Moving pieces together, rather than spreading them out, is safest where the opponent lurks nearby.
\* \*\*Spend bonus throws well.\*\* Rolls of 1, 4, and 5 grant extra throws, and a long chain can be decisive. Within a chain, weigh which piece gains most from each roll.
\* \*\*Build a wall.\*\* Three or more pieces in a row are impassable. A blockade in the right place can strand your opponent for several turns.
\* \*\*Swap with intent.\*\* A capture sends the opponent's piece back to where yours stood. The further behind your piece was, the more the swap stings them — and the more you should avoid leaving lone pieces where the opponent would profit from the same trade.
\* \*\*The endgame is a game of exact rolls.\*\* Squares 28, 29, and 30 each need a specific throw to bear off. Holding pieces on different locked squares lets you put almost any roll to use.
