\*\*Coup\*\*



Coup is an intense game of deduction, deception, and political maneuvering. You are the head of a family vying for power in a corrupt city. Your goal is to be the last player standing by eliminating the influence of your rivals and forcing them into exile. In Coup, bluffing is not just an option, it is often a necessity.



\*\*Gameplay\*\*



The game is played with a deck of 15 cards, consisting of 3 copies of each of the 5 characters: Duke, Assassin, Captain, Ambassador, and Contessa. 



Every player starts with 2 coins and 2 face-down character cards (your "influences"). You are eliminated from the game when you lose both of your cards. 



On your turn, you may perform exactly \*\*one\*\* action. If the action requires a specific character, you must claim that you have that character. You do not have to actually have the card — you just have to claim it!



\*\*Standard Actions (No character required)\*\*



\* \*\*Income:\*\* Take 1 coin from the treasury. This action is perfectly safe and cannot be blocked.

\* \*\*Foreign Aid:\*\* Take 2 coins from the treasury. This can be blocked by anyone claiming they have the Duke.

\* \*\*Coup:\*\* Pay 7 coins to launch a Coup against another player. That player immediately loses one card. A Coup cannot be blocked or challenged. 



\*\*Character Actions\*\*



\* \*\*Duke (Tax):\*\* Take 3 coins from the treasury.

\* \*\*Assassin (Assassinate):\*\* Pay 3 coins and choose a target player. If successful, that player loses a card. This can be blocked by the Contessa.

\* \*\*Captain (Steal):\*\* Steal 2 coins from another player. If they only have 1 coin, steal 1. This can be blocked by the Captain or the Ambassador.

\* \*\*Ambassador (Exchange):\*\* Draw 2 cards from the deck, look at them along with your current face-down cards, and return exactly 2 cards to the deck.



\*\*Challenges and Blocks (The Interrupt Window)\*\*



Whenever a player claims a character to perform an action or to block an action, a brief timer starts. During this interrupt window, any other player can react:



\* \*\*Challenging:\*\* If you think someone is bluffing, you can challenge them. 

&nbsp;   \* If they were \*\*telling the truth\*\*, they reveal the claimed card, shuffle it back into the deck, and draw a new card. You, the challenger, lose a card for being wrong!

&nbsp;   \* If they were \*\*bluffing\*\*, they fail to perform their action and must lose a card.

\* \*\*Blocking:\*\* Some actions can be blocked by specific characters. This block is a claim itself, meaning anyone can now challenge the person who is trying to block!



\*\*Game Options\*\*



The host can configure these settings at the table before starting:

\* \*\*Mandatory Coup Coin Threshold:\*\* The number of coins at which a player is forced to take the Coup action (default is 10, configurable up to 20).

\* \*\*Interrupt Timer Duration:\*\* How long the window stays open for players to Challenge or Block (configurable from 3 to 15 seconds).



\*\*Example Turn\*\*



1\. \*\*Action:\*\* Alice claims she has the Duke and takes 3 coins for Tax.

2\. \*\*Interrupt Window:\*\* The timer starts. Bob thinks Alice is lying and presses \*\*C\*\* to Challenge.

3\. \*\*Resolution:\*\* \* If Alice \*does\* have the Duke, she reveals it. Bob loses a card, and Alice gets her 3 coins.

&nbsp;   \* If Alice \*does not\* have the Duke, her action fails, she gets zero coins, and she loses a card.



\*\*Keyboard Shortcuts\*\*



\* \*\*C:\*\* Challenge a claim or a block.

\* \*\*V:\*\* Block an action targeted at you (or block Foreign Aid).

\* \*\*P:\*\* Pass (agreeing not to challenge or block). If everyone passes, the timer fast-forwards.

\* \*\*W:\*\* Check Wealth (lists all alive players and their coins).

\* \*\*H:\*\* Check Hand (reminds you of your current face-down cards).

\* \*\*L:\*\* Check Table (lists all the eliminated, revealed cards on the table).
