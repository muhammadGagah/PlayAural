\*\*Snakes and Ladders\*\*

Snakes and Ladders is a luck-based race for 2 to 4 players. Each player has one piece and rolls a six-sided die to travel from the starting area to square 100.

Ladders carry you forward. Snakes send you backward. The first player to reach square 100 wins.

\*\*Starting the Game\*\*

Every piece begins in the starting area, just before square 1. Your first roll moves your piece onto the numbered board. This means that rolling 1 as your first move lands on the ladder at square 1 and immediately carries you to square 38.

More than one piece may occupy the same square. Landing on another player does not move or capture either piece.

\*\*Taking a Turn\*\*

Choose \*\*Roll die\*\*. Your piece moves forward by the number rolled.

\* If you land on an ordinary square, your turn ends there.

\* If you land exactly at the foot of a ladder, climb to the square at its top.

\* If you land exactly on a snake's head, slide to the square at its tail.

\* With the default rules, rolling 6 gives you another turn after all movement, ladders, and snakes have been resolved. You continue taking extra turns for as long as you keep rolling 6.

You use a ladder or snake only when your move finishes on its starting square. Passing over one has no effect.

\*\*Reaching Square 100\*\*

You may always win by landing exactly on square 100. The ladder from square 80 also reaches square 100 and wins immediately.

The host chooses one of two finish rules:

\* \*\*Bounce back, default:\*\* If a roll would pass square 100, move to 100 and then move backward by the unused part of the roll. Resolve any snake or ladder on the square where the bounce ends. For example, from square 97, a roll of 4 ends on square 99.

\* \*\*Exact roll; stay put after an overshoot:\*\* If a roll is larger than the number needed to reach square 100, your piece does not move. For example, from square 97, only a roll of 3 wins. A roll of 4, 5, or 6 leaves you on square 97.

Under either finish rule, a roll of 6 still grants another turn when the \*\*Extra turn on 6\*\* option is enabled, even if an overshoot leaves the piece in place.

\*\*Board Layout\*\*

\* \*\*Ladders:\*\* 1 to 38, 4 to 14, 9 to 31, 21 to 42, 28 to 84, 36 to 44, 51 to 67, 71 to 91, and 80 to 100.

\* \*\*Snakes:\*\* 16 to 6, 47 to 26, 49 to 11, 56 to 53, 62 to 19, 64 to 60, 87 to 24, 93 to 73, 95 to 75, and 98 to 78.

\*\*Customizable Options\*\*

\* \*\*Finish rule:\*\* Choose \*\*Bounce back\*\*, the default, or \*\*Exact roll; stay put after an overshoot\*\*.

\* \*\*Extra turn on 6:\*\* Enabled by default. When enabled, rolling 6 gives the same player another turn after the move is fully resolved.

\*\*Personal Game Options\*\*

\* \*\*Brief Announcements:\*\* Uses shorter movement, ladder, snake, bounce, and missed-finish messages while preserving every important square and die result.

\*\*Checking the Race\*\*

\*\*Check positions\*\* opens a live panel showing the finish rule and every active player's current square, distance from the finish, and whose turn it is. The panel stays current while movement is being resolved.

\*\*Keyboard Shortcuts\*\*

\* \*\*R or Space:\*\* Roll the die.

\* \*\*C:\*\* Check positions.

\* \*\*T:\*\* Check whose turn it is.
