\*\*Backgammon\*\*

Backgammon is one of the oldest racing board games in the world, with origins thought to reach back more than four thousand years. Two players guide fifteen checkers each around a shared track of twenty-four points, trying to bring all of their checkers home and bear them off before the opponent can do the same.

\*\*Starting Position\*\*

Each player starts with fifteen checkers in the standard opening arrangement:

\* 2 checkers on your 24-point
\* 5 checkers on your 13-point
\* 3 checkers on your 8-point
\* 5 checkers on your 6-point

Colors are assigned randomly at the start of each match. One player is Red and the other is White.

\*\*Board Layout\*\*

The board is displayed as a grid from your perspective, with your home area in the bottom-right corner. Points are numbered 1 through 6 along the bottom-right, continuing 7 through 12 along the bottom-left, then 13 through 18 along the top-left, and 19 through 24 along the top-right. Movement follows this horseshoe shape: your checkers travel from the high-numbered points down toward your home board at points 1 through 6. Your opponent travels in the opposite direction.

\*\*Gameplay\*\*

Each game begins with an opening roll. Both players roll one die, and the higher roll goes first; ties are re-rolled until someone wins. Those two opening numbers are then used immediately as that player's first turn.

After that, players take turns rolling two dice and moving their checkers according to the numbers rolled. Each die is a separate move. If the dice are different, you normally must use both if legal moves exist. If you roll doubles, you play that number four times. Roll by pressing Enter on any board point.

The turn menu presents the legal moves available at that moment. You continue choosing moves until all usable dice have been spent or no legal move remains. If only one die can legally be played, the higher die must be used.

\*\*Movement and Hitting\*\*

To move a checker, first select the point it is on, then select the destination point. The game automatically matches your move to the correct die. You may move onto:

\* An empty point.
\* A point occupied by your own checkers.
\* A point occupied by exactly one opposing checker.

If you land on a point with exactly one opposing checker, that checker is hit and sent to the bar.

You may not move onto a point occupied by two or more opposing checkers. Those points are blocked.

If you have any checker on the bar, you must re-enter that checker before moving any others. When you have a checker on the bar, simply select any destination point and the game moves from the bar automatically. A bar checker re-enters onto an open point in your opponent's home board, using the dice just like any other move. If you have checkers on the bar and cannot enter, your entire turn is forfeit.

\*\*Bearing Off\*\*

Once all fifteen of your checkers are in your home board, you may begin bearing them off. To bear off, select the checker and then select the same point again, or press Enter twice on it. An exact roll bears off a checker directly. If you roll higher than your highest occupied point, you may bear off from that highest point. You may not use an overshooting die to bear off from a lower point if any checker remains on a higher point.

If a checker is hit during bearing off, it must go to the bar, re-enter, and travel all the way back home before you can resume bearing off.

\*\*Scoring\*\*

The winner of a game scores match points:

\* \*\*1 point\*\* for a normal win, if the loser has already borne off at least one checker.
\* \*\*2 points\*\* for a gammon, if the loser has borne off none.
\* \*\*3 points\*\* for a backgammon, if the loser has borne off none and still has a checker on the bar or in the winner's home board.

If the doubling cube has been used, the final value of the cube multiplies the score for that game.

\*\*Doubling Cube and Crawford Rule\*\*

In matches longer than one point, the active player may offer a double before rolling. If the opponent accepts, the cube value doubles and ownership of the cube passes to the accepting player, who is then the only one who may offer the next double. If the opponent drops, the offering player wins the current game immediately for the cube's current value.

The Crawford rule is applied automatically. When a player is exactly one point away from winning the match, the next game becomes a Crawford game and the doubling cube is disabled for that game. After the Crawford game, doubling resumes for all subsequent games. The doubling cube is not available in single games (match length of 1).

\*\*Match Play\*\*

A match consists of multiple games played to a target score. After each game, the winner's points are added to their match score. The first player to reach or exceed the target wins the match. Colors remain the same throughout the match, but a new opening roll determines who goes first in each game. The doubling cube resets to centered at 1 for each new game.

\*\*Game Options\*\*

\* \*\*Match Length:\*\* Default 1. Range 1 to 25. This is the number of points needed to win the match. A value of 1 is a single game with no doubling cube.
\* \*\*Bot Difficulty:\*\* Default Simple. Choices: Simple or Random.

\*\*Keyboard Shortcuts\*\*

\* \*\*Enter (on any grid point):\*\* Roll the dice before rolling, or select and move a checker during movement.
\* \*\*Ctrl+Backspace:\*\* Deselect the currently selected checker.
\* \*\*Ctrl+Down or Ctrl+Right:\*\* Cycle forward through navigation targets. With a checker selected, cycles through available destinations (opponent blots first). Without a selection, cycles through source points that have legal moves.
\* \*\*Ctrl+Up or Ctrl+Left:\*\* Cycle backward through navigation targets.
\* \*\*Shift+D:\*\* Offer a double before rolling (match play only).
\* \*\*Y:\*\* Accept an offered double.
\* \*\*N:\*\* Drop an offered double.
\* \*\*U:\*\* Undo the last sub-move of the current turn.
\* \*\*E:\*\* Read the checker counts on the bar and already borne off.
\* \*\*P:\*\* Read the pip counts for both players.
\* \*\*D:\*\* Read the doubling cube status.
\* \*\*S:\*\* Read the current match score.
\* \*\*C:\*\* Read the remaining dice for the current turn.
